Circles… round, repetitive… same thing every time.
Do you ever feel like you’re just rotating—same pattern, no space for wonder? Have you? Maybe… can’t be 100% sure, you know.

So how do you get out of this trance?
This repetitiveness that seems to lead nowhere… that keeps you stuck in a box?
How do you actually break out?
How do you think outside it… this caged, programmed mentality that’s been implanted in us?

Honestly… I don’t fully know.

But what I do know is this—I don’t want to spend years stuck in the same cycle over and over again. I don’t want to live in this constant chase for money, this grind where money starts controlling who I am. I want to be the master of my own life… my own person.

And maybe, sometimes, it’s just about finding purpose.
Not in that heavy way like, “Oh, what should I study in uni?” or “What career should I pursue?”
Nah… start simpler than that.

What do you love?

Let’s start there.
What’s your passion? Your hobby?
Because that… that’s your power. That’s what makes you you.

From there, you can build something.

It doesn’t mean education is useless—it just means we need to connect the two: your hobbies and your education. You need to understand that what you love isn’t a distraction from your future… it might actually be your future. You just have to find a way to integrate the two.

Personally, I’ve always loved computers—how they work, how you can create systems that solve problems, just understanding how things function. I love that.

I think that’s why, as a kid, I could never keep a watch or toy intact—I’d always open it up and inspect everything inside. And when I got a laptop? That was it. That’s where my interest really locked in. I clicked everything, explored anything, just trying to understand.

Yeah, I got scolded a lot for it. Told not to mess around.
But my mind just couldn’t stop.

Because that’s my passion… so why stop me?

So computers became my thing. But even now, I’m still trying to figure out which part of it I want to go into. And that’s okay. It’s not something you figure out overnight—it takes time. But patience matters… and experience? That’s the best teacher.

So try things out. Explore. Find yourself.

And maybe—just maybe—in that process, you’ll finally break out of that cycle… that circle of repetition.
